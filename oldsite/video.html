Video Archive

  
 

We interviewed Taylor Ross, one of the founders and organizers of The Beauty Shop, a DIY space and music residency program in Fairfield, Iowa, in late August 2012.

We interviewed and filmed Paper Bird in a sunny community garden in Denver, Colorado in mid-August, 2012, and Esme, Genny and Sarah sang an acapella version of "The Doldrums" for us.

Saintseneca's Maryn and Zac performed "Only the Young Die Good" in their home in Columbus, Ohio in early August, 2012.

Folk to Folk got the chance to hang out with and film The Wild at a Red Roof Inn in Gainesville during the Fest 10 last October.

We filmed and interviewed Yo Soybean at their practice space in Athens, Georgia in late October, 2011.

Folk to Folk spent a few days in Asheville, North Carolina in late October 2011 and got the chance to film The Honeycutters and Uncle Mountain at their homes.

Our first stop on our Fall 2011 road trip was the Baltimore home of Ryan Harvey and Mark Gunnery, two members of the Riot Folk Collective.

We met Alynda and Yosi of Hurray for the Riff Raff by the Mississippi River in the Lower 9th Ward of New Orleans on the Day of the Dead 2011. They played "Lookout Mama," the title track from their newest album and a beautiful cover of Lucinda Williams' "People Talkin'." We also spoke to them about the culture and community of their adopted city before heading off to a parade to mourn friends lost in the past year. Footage of our interview with them will be featured in our documentary.

We caught up with New York’s Pearl and the Beard right before they played a great set at Kitchen Sessions’ and Visible Voice’s Newport Nightcap afterparty for the Newport Folk Festival 2011.

We first met Mornin' Old Sport last summer when we filmed a pop-up performance video for the Boston Phoenix with them on a rainy day in downtown. We later did an interview with them in our shared favorite old stomping grounds, Allston, Mass. Soon after, Mornin' Old Sport took off for the West Coast, released their first full album, got written up in American Songwriter, recorded at Daytrotter and went on a two-month long cross country tour. When we saw them again, it was at this summer's Allston DIY Fest, full of good vibes and love.

Folk to Folk spoke with the Hudson Valley's Sons of an Illustrious Father on an uncomfortably hot late July 2011 evening before their show at a beloved DIY venue in Allston, Mass.

Jamaica Plain's Gracious Calamity were our first subjects back in June 2011. Kit and Kate gave us a great interview and as we've since learned to record live music much more effectively, we hope to one day have a wonderful accompanying performance video for this episode.
